9:Tb1f,<h2>About</h2>
<p>The Benelux Algorithmic Programming Contest (BAPC) is a 4 hour competitive programming contest, where students form teams of 1 to 3 people. The target is to solve the most problems and win the champion's cup.</p>
<p>The event will take place on the Saturday 4th of May in the Bernoulliborg, from 9:30 - 20:00. This is a full-day event, where not only the lunch, dinner and snacks will be provided, but also you'll have the chance to interact with your fellow contestants and feel the ICPC-style event vibe. More details about the schedule can be found <a href="/schedule/">here</a>.</p>
<h2>Contest</h2>
<p>This contest is open to all students from the University of Groningen. The contest machine will be a laptop provided by the organisers. Moreover, the teams are allowed to bring a Team Contest Reference (TCR), one non-programmable wired mouse and/or keyboard. These will be given to be checked by the jury in the registration session (9:30-10:30). More details about the rules can be found <a href="/rules/">here</a>.</p>
<p>To solve the problems, you will be able to use Java, Python, C and C++. In the end, only the captain of the team needs to make the registration and it can be done <a href="/register/">here</a>.</p>
